Key Takeaways for Leadership

1.Credit cards are the growth engine. India will add roughly 8 million new credit cards over the next year, reaching approximately 126 million by February 2027. Growth has moderated from 25% annually to around 7%, primarily because RBI tightened unsecured lending rules in late 2023, but the trajectory remains firmly upward.
2.Debit cards are being displaced, but not disappearing. Nearly a billion debit cards remain in circulation (986 million forecast for Feb 2027), but people are barely using them. Debit card transactions have collapsed 93% since 2019 as consumers shifted to UPI for everyday payments. The cards themselves stay in wallets largely because of salary account linkage and ATM access, not active merchant usage.
3.UPI now processes more transactions in one month than credit and debit cards combined process in a year. At 23 billion monthly transactions and growing at roughly 15% annually, UPI dominates the Indian payments landscape. For any card product strategy, the question is not whether to compete with UPI, but how to complement it.
4.The top five banks control over 60% of the credit card market. HDFC Bank (24M cards), SBI (21M), ICICI (18M), Axis (15M), and Kotak (5M) together represent the bulk of issuance. For MPi, this concentration means that a single issuer's strategic shift -- tightening underwriting, launching a new product, or partnering on UPI credit -- moves the national numbers.
5.RuPay credit on UPI is the wild card. If the RBI's push to enable credit cards on the UPI rail gains traction, it could meaningfully accelerate credit card issuance among banks that have so far focused only on debit. This is the single biggest upside risk to our credit card forecast, and we are awaiting internal confirmation on whether these transactions are already reflected in the outstanding figures.
